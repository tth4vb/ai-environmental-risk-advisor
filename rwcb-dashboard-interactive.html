About this metric
What This Measures

The number of seedlings or saplings produced in nurseries and ready for planting. This is an early pipeline indicator that tracks nursery capacity and preparation—relevant primarily before planting operations begin.

Why It Matters

Champions need to ensure adequate seedling supply to meet planting targets. A shortfall here could delay planting schedules. However, this metric has high risk of misinterpretation—seedlings produced ≠ trees planted ≠ ecological outcomes. This is supply chain logistics, not conservation impact.

Key Questions This Helps Answer
  • Are nurseries on track to supply planting needs for the upcoming season?
  • Do we need to ramp up nursery production or engage additional suppliers?
  • Is the seedling mix aligned with species diversity targets?

About this metric
What This Measures

The percentage of planted trees still alive at measurement time. Measured annually (with early checks encouraged in Year 1). This is the highest-value decision indicator for assessing whether restoration interventions are working.

Why It Matters

Survival rate reveals the effectiveness of site preparation, species selection, planting techniques, and ongoing maintenance. It's the bridge between implementation (planting) and outcomes (restored hectares).

Interpretation challenges: Early survival is highly volatile due to transplant shock, weather variation, and establishment phase dynamics. Early rates are NOT predictive of final outcomes. Champions risk over-reacting to normal early fluctuations if trends aren't properly contextualized.

Key Questions This Helps Answer
  • Are our restoration interventions effective or at risk?
  • Do specific sites, species, or cohorts need intervention (replanting, additional maintenance)?
  • Are early mortality patterns normal or indicative of systematic problems?
  • Will we likely meet the final restoration target given current survival trends?

About this metric
What This Measures

The number of trees that have naturally sprouted and are growing in restoration areas without being planted. This captures Assisted Natural Regeneration (ANR) success—where restoration activities create conditions for native vegetation to return on its own.

Data comes as both numeric counts (annual) and qualitative evidence with photos (bi-annual), recognizing that natural regeneration is harder to measure precisely than planted trees.

Why It Matters

Natural regeneration is often more cost-effective and ecologically appropriate than planting. It demonstrates that restoration is catalyzing ecosystem recovery beyond direct planting efforts. High natural regeneration can indicate good site conditions and effective land management.

Measurement uncertainty: Counts are estimates with high uncertainty. It's difficult to distinguish young naturally regenerated trees from planted ones, and detection depends on tree size. Risk of overstating confidence if shown as precise numbers.

Key Questions This Helps Answer
  • Is ANR delivering the expected ecological results?
  • Can we reduce planting intensity in areas with strong natural regeneration?
  • Which sites or interventions are most successful at catalyzing natural recovery?
  • Are our ANR protocols (e.g., fencing, soil management) working?

What This Measures

A composite index measuring the variety of native tree species present across restoration sites. Combines species richness (number of species) with evenness (balanced distribution) to assess biodiversity recovery.

About this metric
What This Measures

The total hectares that meet defined restoration success criteria at the end of Year 5. This is the bond outcome metric—the final measure of whether restoration commitments were achieved.

Why It Matters

This is the ultimate accountability measure. Bond performance payments are tied to verified hectares restored. Unlike progress indicators (planting, survival), this measures final verified outcomes.

Critical Design Requirement

Two distinct states:

  • Pre-verification (Years 1-5): Shows timeline to verification. NO projections or estimates allowed—would create false expectations.
  • Post-verification (Year 5+): Shows verified outcome with independent validation badge. Final, auditable number.

Why no projections? Showing projected outcomes would undermine the verification process and create expectations that may not be met. The dashboard shows progress indicators (planting, survival, etc.) but does not extrapolate to final outcomes.
